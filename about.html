Goran S. Milovanovic, Phd

  

I founded DataKolektiv in 2017, following almost twenty-five years of my career in academic and applied research. In 2017-2022 we provided full-stack data science for Wikidata, the largest and arguably the world’s most complex open knowledge base: from ETL/pre-processing in Big Data systems, Machine Learning, custom statistical analyses, towards hand-crafted dashboards in virtualized production environments. Here is my Wikidata Analytics System repo. During this period, DataKolektiv has also been at the forefront of innovation, offering expert consulting services to premier Swiss banking institutions and leading global technology-oriented market research firms. We’ve also played a pivotal role in enhancing the skills of numerous professionals in Data Analytics, Data Science, and Machine Learning across local and Western European markets. We have educated many in AI development, particularly since the emergence of Generative AI systems, positioning ourselves as one of the earliest consultancies to embrace this new technological era. From 2022 to 2026, I served as a Senior and Lead Data Scientist, and finally as a Chief AI Officer in smartocto, leading a team of data scientists, analysts, and engineers, designing and developing ML and AI supported products in media analytics.

In my professional and academic career, I have educated hundreds in methodology, statistics, psychology, Data Science, and AI. I have been programming since 1984, and that would be the 8-bit era. When I was sixteen, I have published my first review of a PASCAL compiler, and wrote my first scientific paper when I was nineteen. Since then, I have been working at the intersection of computer science, mathematical statistics, and social sciences. I have co-edited and co-authored five books on Internet user behavior and attitudes, encompassing theoretical aspects of the development of Information Society and Internet Governance. In my academic track I have done a lot of work on human associative and semantic memory, concepts and categorization, statistical causal induction, and judgment and decision making under risk.
